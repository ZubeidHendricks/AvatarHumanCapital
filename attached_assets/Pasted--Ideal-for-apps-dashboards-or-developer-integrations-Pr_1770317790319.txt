### Short answer

**Almost none of the platforms we discussed (jobs, candidates, C-suite data) are open source.**
They are **commercial data businesses**.

But 👇 here’s the clear breakdown so you know exactly where you stand.

---

## ✅ **Actually Open Source (you can self-host / modify)**

These are **frameworks, engines, or building blocks**, not data providers.

### 🧠 Recruiting / Talent Tech (Open Source)

* **OpenCATS** – Open-source ATS (older but real)
* **HireHive OSS forks** – Community-maintained ATS variants
* **ERPNext HR** – Open-source HR + recruitment module
* **Odoo Community Edition** – Recruitment module (core is OSS)

👉 These manage **your own candidates**, not external ones.

---

### 🔍 Search / Matching / Parsing (Open Source)

* **Elasticsearch / OpenSearch** – Candidate & executive search
* **spaCy** – NLP for CV parsing
* **Apache Tika** – Resume document parsing
* **Haystack** – Semantic search / matching
* **MLflow** – ML pipelines for candidate scoring

---

### 🕸️ Scraping / Data Collection (Open Source)

* **Scrapy** – Web scraping framework
* **Playwright** – Browser automation
* **Puppeteer**
* **Selenium**

⚠️ Legal to use, but **what you scrape may not be legal to redistribute**.

---

## ❌ **NOT Open Source (Commercial / Closed)**

### 🚫 Job APIs

* APIJobs
* jobdataAPI
* Indeed
* Adzuna

➡️ **Paid access, proprietary datasets**

---

### 🚫 Candidate Databases

* Pearch AI
* DataVertex
* BoardEx
* LinkedIn data (via any provider)

➡️ These sell **access to people**, which is why they’re never open source.

---

### 🚫 C-Suite Networks & Forums

* CEO Circle
* C-Suite Nexus
* Top Executive
* Southern Africa CEO Forum

➡️ **Membership-based communities**, not software.

---

## 🧩 The Important Reality (Straight talk)

> **Open source ≠ free data**

* Software can be open source
* **Data about people almost never is** (privacy, POPIA, GDPR, commercial value)

That’s why:

* Job boards sell access
* Candidate databases are closed
* Executive data is licensed, not shared
